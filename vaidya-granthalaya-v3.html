The Three Cosmic Forces

Every living being is a unique confluence of three biological energies. Click a dosha card to reveal its deep secrets.

✦✦
💨

Vata

वात

Air · Ether · Space · Movement

The energy of movement, breath, and communication. Quick, creative, and ever-changing like the wind across open sky.

DryLightColdSubtleMobile

Imbalance Symptoms

Anxiety, restlessness, dry skin, constipation, insomnia, fear, tremors, scattered thinking, forgetfulness.

Balancing Foods

Warm, oily, heavy foods. Ghee, sesame oil, sweet potatoes, rice, wheat, dairy, nuts, cooked vegetables, warm soups.

Daily Routine

Regular meal times, warm oil massage (Abhyanga), gentle yoga, early sleep by 10 PM, minimal screen time, grounding practices.

Yoga for Vata

Slow, grounding poses — Tadasana, Balasana, Shavasana. Avoid fast-paced sequences. Focus on stillness and breath retention.

Sacred Herbs

Ashwagandha, Shatavari, Bala, Vidari Kanda, warm sesame oil, Triphala at night, ginger tea each morning.

✦✦
🔥

Pitta

पित्त

Fire · Water · Transformation

The energy of transformation, metabolism, and intelligence. Sharp, penetrating, and purposeful like the sacred temple flame.

HotSharpOilyIntenseFluid

Imbalance Symptoms

Anger, irritability, inflammation, heartburn, acidity, skin rashes, excessive heat, perfectionism, jealousy.

Balancing Foods

Cooling, sweet, bitter foods. Coconut milk, cucumber, leafy greens, sweet fruits, ghee, coriander, fennel, mint.

Daily Routine

Avoid midday sun, eat lunch as largest meal, practice compassion, take cool walks near water, protect from overwork.

Yoga for Pitta

Cooling, relaxing poses — Chandra Namaskar, Bhujangasana, Supta Baddha Konasana. Sheetali pranayama always.

Sacred Herbs

Amalaki, Brahmi, Shatavari, Manjistha, rose water, aloe vera gel, coriander tea, neem leaf, guduchi.

✦✦
🌊

Kapha

कफ

Earth · Water · Structure · Form

The energy of structure, cohesion, and nurturing. Steady, enduring, and fertile like the sacred earth from which all life emerges.

HeavySlowCoolSoftDense

Imbalance Symptoms

Lethargy, weight gain, congestion, attachment, depression, sluggish digestion, excessive sleep, possessiveness.

Balancing Foods

Light, dry, warm foods. Barley, millet, bitter greens, pungent spices, honey, legumes, ginger, black pepper.

Daily Routine

Rise before sunrise, vigorous daily exercise, avoid daytime napping, seek new experiences, reduce heavy foods.

Yoga for Kapha

Energizing, active flows — Surya Namaskar, Virabhadrasana, Ustrasana. Bhastrika pranayama vigorously.

Sacred Herbs

Trikatu, Guggul, Punarnava, Chitrak, Triphala, ginger, black pepper, dry ginger tea, kutki, shilajit.

The Sacred Science of Yoga

Yoga is the living companion of Ayurveda — the path that brings the body's energies into divine harmony with the cosmos.

🧘

Dosha Balance Through Asana

Each posture activates specific pranas. Grounding poses pacify Vata, cooling postures soothe Pitta, invigorating sequences awaken Kapha — restoring sacred equilibrium.

🌬️

Pranayama — Control of Life Force

The science of breath regulation purifies 72,000 nadis, awakening dormant prana, calming the nervous system, and igniting the sacred digestive fire (Agni).

🧠

Stress Reduction & Mental Clarity

Consistent yoga practice reduces cortisol, calms the amygdala, and cultivates the sattvic quality of mind essential for all healing and spiritual growth.

🔥

Igniting Digestive Fire (Agni)

Twisting postures and specific pranayamas massage abdominal organs, stoking the sacred digestive fire that Ayurveda places at the center of all health.

🌸

Inner Peace & Spiritual Connection

Meditation awakens the witness consciousness described in Vedic texts as the source of all health, clarity, and liberation from suffering.

दिनचर्या — Daily Sacred Rhythm

The Ayurvedic Day

Alignment with nature's rhythms is the foundation of health. The Dinacharya keeps all three doshas in sacred balance throughout each day.

🌄

4:00 – 6:00 AM · Brahma Muhurta

Rise Before the Sun

Wake during the hour of Brahma — when cosmic energy is most sattvic. Sit quietly, offer gratitude, observe the sacred silence before the world awakens.

🦷

6:00 – 7:00 AM · Cleansing Rituals

Oral & Body Purification

Oil pull with sesame oil (15 min). Scrape the tongue. Wash face with cold water. Drink warm water with lemon. Nasya (nasal oil). Neem twig for teeth.

🧘

7:00 – 8:00 AM · Sacred Movement

Yoga & Meditation

Begin with dosha-appropriate yoga. Follow with pranayama (Nadi Shodhana for Vata, Sheetali for Pitta, Kapalabhati for Kapha). Close with 15–20 min meditation.

🍵

8:00 – 9:00 AM · Morning Nourishment

The Sacred First Meal

Light, warm, easily digestible. Herbal tea suited to your dosha. Warm porridge or soaked fruits. Never eat while distracted. Sit facing east. Offer a prayer.

☀️

12:00 – 1:00 PM · Midday Meal

The Largest Sacred Meal

Pitta peaks at noon — digestive fire is strongest. Eat the main meal of the day in calm surroundings. Chew thoroughly. Rest 5–10 minutes after eating.

🌿

3:00 – 5:00 PM · Vata Peak

Afternoon Restoration

Vata increases in late afternoon. Sip warm herbal tea. Avoid stimulants. Take a short walk in nature. Ideal for creative work, journaling, or light reading.

🌙

6:00 – 7:00 PM · Evening Meal

Sunset Nourishment

The lightest meal of the day. Warm soups, kitchari, or cooked vegetables. Avoid heavy proteins at night. Digestive fire diminishes after sunset — honor this truth.

🕯️

9:00 – 10:00 PM · Sacred Sleep

The Path to Sacred Sleep

Oil the feet and scalp with warm sesame oil. Read sacred texts or practice gratitude. Dim all lights. Avoid screens. Warm milk with ashwagandha. Sleep by 10 PM.

आहार विज्ञान — Sacred Food Science

Ayurvedic Diet Wisdom

Food is the first medicine. Every morsel carries a quality (guna) and a taste (rasa) that affects consciousness, energy, and dosha balance.

Sattvic Foods (सात्विक)

Pure, light, life-giving — the food of sages. Promotes clarity, compassion, and spiritual awakening.

Grains & Legumes

  • White rice, barley, oats, mung dal
  • Fresh whole wheat, amaranth, quinoa
  • Freshly cooked lentils and chickpeas

Fruits & Vegetables

  • Sweet fresh fruits — mangoes, figs, dates
  • Root vegetables, leafy greens, cucumber
  • Fresh coconut and coconut milk

Benefits & Sacred Practices

  • Promotes lightness, mental clarity, and joy
  • Supports meditation and spiritual practice
  • Naturally balances all three doshas
  • Eaten with awareness and gratitude
  • Always fresh — never leftover or processed

Sattvic Spices

  • Turmeric, cumin, coriander, fennel
  • Cardamom, saffron, fresh ginger
  • Fresh herbs — tulsi, mint, cilantro

Rajasic Foods (राजसिक)

Stimulating and passion-provoking. Increases Pitta and Vata. Suitable in moderation for active individuals.

Stimulating Foods

  • Strong spices — chili, black pepper, mustard
  • Coffee, tea, chocolate, energy drinks
  • Onion, garlic, leek, shallot
  • Meat — especially poultry and fish

When Rajasic is Appropriate

  • Athletes and highly active individuals
  • Kapha types who need stimulation
  • During intense work or study periods
  • In cold seasons to generate internal heat

Balancing Rajasic Tendencies

  • Pair with cooling herbs — coriander, mint
  • Practice cooling yoga and pranayama daily
  • Spend time in nature and near water

Tamasic Foods (तामसिक)

Heavy, dull, obstructing. Promotes lethargy and disease. Increases Ama. Avoid as much as possible.

Foods to Minimize

  • Leftover and reheated foods (next-day)
  • Processed, canned, and packaged foods
  • Alcohol, tobacco, recreational substances
  • Deep-fried and excessively oily foods

Effects of Tamas

  • Mental dullness, confusion, lack of clarity
  • Excessive sleep, lethargy, depression
  • Accumulation of Ama (digestive toxins)
  • Weakening of Ojas (vital immune essence)

Purification Path

  • Panchakarma — seasonal deep detoxification
  • Gradual transition to Sattvic foods
  • Morning hot water with lemon and ginger
  • Triphala churna — nightly digestive cleanser

Summer & Monsoon

Grishma (Summer)

  • Cool, sweet, liquid-rich foods
  • Coconut water, buttermilk, fresh fruits
  • Avoid spicy, sour, and salty foods

Varsha (Monsoon)

  • Light, easily digestible foods
  • Avoid raw vegetables and leafy greens
  • Warm soups, cooked grains, ginger tea

Autumn & Winter

Sharad (Autumn)

  • Sweet, bitter, and astringent tastes
  • Pomegranate, amla, leafy greens
  • Cooling herbs — coriander, sandalwood

Hemanta/Shishira (Winter)

  • Heavy, oily, warm, and nourishing foods
  • Sesame, jaggery, ghee, root vegetables
  • Warm spiced milk — best Rasayana season

सूक्तियाँ — Ancient Wisdom

Sacred Thoughts from the Archive

हिताहितं सुखं दुःखमायुस्तस्य हिताहितम् ।
मानं च तच्च यत्रोक्तमायुर्वेदः स उच्यते ॥

"That science which declares what is beneficial and harmful to life, what is joyful and sorrowful — that is Ayurveda."

— Charaka Samhita, Sutrasthana 1.41

शरीरं आद्यं खलु धर्मसाधनम् ।

"The body is indeed the primary instrument for fulfilling all duties and purposes of life. Without health, dharma, wealth, and liberation cannot be pursued."

— Kalidasa, Kumārasambhava 5.33

आहारः प्रथमो वैद्यः ।

"Food is the first physician. What we consume becomes our body, our mind, and our consciousness. The wise prescribe proper diet before all medicines."

— Ancient Ayurvedic Aphorism

योगश्चित्तवृत्तिनिरोधः ।

"Yoga is the cessation of the fluctuations of the mind. When the mind becomes still as a lamp in a windless place, one experiences the self beyond suffering."

— Yoga Sutras of Patanjali, 1.2

सर्वे भवन्तु सुखिनः ।
सर्वे सन्तु निरामयाः ॥

"May all beings be happy. May all beings be free from disease. May all beings witness auspiciousness, and may none suffer."

— Brihadaranyaka Upanishad

स्वस्थस्य स्वास्थ्यरक्षणं आतुरस्य विकारप्रशमनं च ।

"To preserve the health of the healthy, and to cure the diseases of the sick — these two are the twin purposes of Ayurveda."

— Sushruta Samhita, Sutrasthana 1.14

आधुनिक संदर्भ — Ancient Wisdom for Modern Times

Ayurveda in Your Daily Life

Ancient wisdom does not fade — it transforms. Ayurveda offers precise answers to the deepest ailments of modern life.

📱

Screen Fatigue & Digital Overload

Excessive screen use is Vata-aggravating — drying the eyes, depleting Ojas, scattering the mind. Nasya oil, eye washing with rose water, and screen-free mornings restore balance.

🍔

Fast Food & Processed Diets

Processed foods are quintessentially Tamasic — dead, depleted of prana, loaded with Ama. Triphala before bed and digestive spices at every meal reset the digestive system.

😰

Chronic Stress & Anxiety

Modern stress is Vata and Pitta imbalance made epidemic. Ashwagandha, Abhyanga self-oil massage, and Nadi Shodhana pranayama are Ayurveda's proven answers.

💤

Sleep Disorders & Insomnia

Ayurveda calls proper sleep (Nidra) one of the three pillars of life. Warm milk with ashwagandha, scalp massage, and sleeping before 10 PM restore natural sleep cycles.

🏙️

Urban Disconnection from Nature

Barefoot walking on earth, morning sun exposure, growing herbs at home, and moon gazing restore the elemental equilibrium modern city life systematically destroys.

🎓

Students & Working Professionals

Brahmi for memory, Triphala for gut-brain support, Yoga Nidra (20 min = 4 hours rest), and Nadi Shodhana before exams — these are ancient secrets for modern success.
